Lighting

What are the differences?

Lighting is very important when it comes to things such as photos and/or films. But what are the different types of lighting? In fact, there are 4 different types of lighitng, which are:

  1. Hard
  2. Soft
  3. Specular
  4. Diffused

Hard Lighting

When someone refers to hard light, they are usually referring to the light that a harsh, but well-defined shadow on what is it illuminating. This would give it a very quick and immediate transition between the highlights and shadows. To create this effect one would use a smaller light than the object so it can create more focus on specific features. This is usually used to create tense scenes.



Soft Lighting

On the other hand, soft light only gives us few hard shadows but is bright and balanced. The transition of highlight to shadow is gradual and similar to a gradient, smooth even.In this case, to get this effect one would need to use a big light and bring it closer to the subject. This lightining helps hide acne, smooth wrinkles and bring out someones eyes. This is usually used in photography like:

  • Fashion
  • Travel
  • Food
  • Portraits


Specular Lighting

If a photographer states that they used specular lighting, then that means that they managed to identify extreamly bright highlights when the light source hits the surface of the object. It then is reflected back to the camera which then show up as a white spot of light. This usually reflects things such as moist and/or oily areas of the face, or extreamly shiny objects. This form of light is used mostly for adding dimention to object to make them look more three dimentional.


Diffused Lighting

Diffuesd lightingis a form of soft light (as explained before) however, the difference is that this form of soft light is filtered by something like, for example, sunlight coming from a sheer curtain. In this case, the photographer could use coloured plastic or a sheet to get the desired look on what they want their photograph to look like. This form of light eliminates shadows and softens the face while also allowing the image to look warmer.
